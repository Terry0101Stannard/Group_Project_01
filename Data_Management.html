Data Management

One of the main things to look at when looking into how best to manage your data is safety. Your data is a snippet of your life, it could be photos from a trip or even passwords for your online portals. Make sure that no matter what, you always follow these tips when storing your data:

  • ALWAYS back up your data on a seperate hard drive.
  • NEVER give your password to anyone.
  • ALWAYS use two-factor authentication when storing your data anywhere.
  • NEVER leave your devices unattended in public/shared spaces
  • ALWAYS use trusted apps to manage your data like secure password managers from trusted brands.

Spreadsheets can be the saviour of all when it comes to managing your personal life. For instance, have you ever signed up for a free trial and then forgotten all about it? Then in the coming months you realise you have spent a lot of money on a product you were never really using? Set up your own finance tracker! It doesn't have to be fancy, however there are thousands of templates online that can help you with this if needed. This can help you clearly see where all of your money is going at any given time. No more hidden payments, put everything in your tracker and better keep on top of things. By visualising your data you are keeping yourself accountable for any finances that may hinder you at a later stage

You can also use this to help you budget. Formulas can be used to help you save money every month. Say for instance you want to save 10 percent of your leftover money every month. You can put in your monthly takings, deduct all of your bills from that to see how much you have left and then ten percent of that can be saved. And the best part is you dont have to work out any of this for yourself! Excel has all the functions you need to turn it into a big calculator, or again, find a template online that fits all of your needs. Sometimes you can even comission people to do it for you!
